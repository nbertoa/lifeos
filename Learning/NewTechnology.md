# Learning New Technology

## Purpose

Learning a new technology is an investment.

The objective is not to adopt every new tool immediately, but to build practical knowledge that can be applied
confidently in real projects.

I prefer understanding over novelty.

---

## Learn From Structured Sources

When learning something new, I begin with structured educational material.

My preferred starting points are:

- High-quality courses.
- Official documentation.
- AI-assisted explanations.

Courses provide context and progression, while official documentation provides accuracy.

AI accelerates understanding by explaining concepts and answering questions that arise while studying.

---

## Prefer Mature Technology

I do not feel the need to adopt every new technology immediately after release.

I prefer waiting until a technology becomes more mature and the community has had time to:

- Discover common problems.
- Establish best practices.
- Produce quality documentation.
- Create courses and tutorials.
- Share real-world experience.

Reducing uncertainty is usually more valuable than being an early adopter.

---

## Build a Test Project

I rarely learn directly inside a production project.

Instead, I create a small test project dedicated to experimentation.

This allows me to:

- Explore features freely.
- Make mistakes safely.
- Understand limitations.
- Validate ideas before using them professionally.

Learning should happen in an environment where experimentation is encouraged.

---

## Combine Multiple Learning Sources

One course is rarely enough.

I often reinforce my understanding through:

- Multiple courses.
- Official documentation.
- AI discussions.
- Personal experimentation.

Different sources provide different perspectives and produce deeper understanding.

---

## Validate Through Practice

Knowledge becomes meaningful when it is applied.

A technology is not truly learned simply because I finished reading or watching a course.

I need to build something with it.

Working software is the best confirmation that understanding is becoming practical.

---

## Continue Learning While Building

Learning does not stop when the first project succeeds.

Even after applying a technology in a real project, I continue experimenting, refining my understanding, and discovering
better approaches.

Every project becomes another learning opportunity.

---

## Learn Progressively

I do not wait until I know everything before using a technology.

Once I have validated the fundamentals in a test project and developed enough confidence through practice, I begin
applying it in real work while continuing to learn.

Experience and learning evolve together.

---

## What Success Looks Like

Successful learning means:

- Beginning with structured educational material.
- Using official documentation to deepen understanding.
- Leveraging AI to accelerate learning.
- Preferring mature technologies over unnecessary novelty.
- Validating concepts in isolated test projects.
- Reinforcing knowledge through multiple sources.
- Applying knowledge through real implementations.
- Continuing to learn throughout professional use.
