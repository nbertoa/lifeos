# LifeOS Architecture

## Vision

LifeOS is a long-term personal knowledge system designed to organize durable knowledge, guide continuous learning, and
enable effective collaboration with AI assistants.

It is intended to evolve over decades while remaining simple, maintainable, and independent of any specific tool or AI
model.

## Design Principles

Keep documents focused, durable, and easy to navigate. Prefer stable knowledge over transient notes, link related
material directly, and distinguish personal guidance from externally sourced practices.

## Repository Structure

The repository is organized into top-level life areas. Each area uses a README as its entry point and contains focused
documents that can be read independently and discovered through relative links.

## Document Types

README files provide navigation and local context. Topic documents capture durable knowledge, principles, practices, or
reference material. Planning documents identify future work without representing it as completed guidance.

## Evolution Strategy

Add knowledge incrementally, revise documents when understanding changes, and remove or consolidate content that has
become redundant. Keep indexes current whenever documents are added, renamed, or retired.

## AI Integration

AI assistants support research, drafting, review, and navigation while human judgment remains responsible for the
accuracy, relevance, and final form of repository content.

## Future Extensions

Future areas may add templates, review checklists, source registries, and periodic maintenance records when they provide
clear long-term value.
