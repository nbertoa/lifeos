# Online Presence

## Purpose

This document defines how my professional identity is represented online. It keeps platform responsibilities clear, maintains a consistent professional narrative, avoids duplicated or contradictory profiles, makes official links easy to identify, and guides future updates.

It is not a directory of every social account or service I use. It records only the professional presence that should be treated as canonical and the principles that keep it accurate over time.

## Professional Presence Model

> LifeOS is the source of truth.
> nbertoa.com is the professional portfolio.
> LinkedIn communicates the professional narrative.
> GitHub provides technical evidence.

Each platform has one primary responsibility. They complement one another instead of competing with one another. Some overlap is useful for discovery and context, but no platform should become a copy of all the others. The appropriate platform should contain the detail; the others should summarize or link to it.

## Platform Responsibilities

### LifeOS

LifeOS is the long-term source of truth for knowledge, engineering principles, decisions, learning, personal operating procedures, and durable professional judgment. It provides depth and continuity for ideas that need to remain useful across projects and career stages.

LifeOS is not primarily a marketing site or portfolio. Its purpose is not first-impression presentation, broad technical coverage, or public activity. Public material may refer to LifeOS when it adds useful context, but the repository should retain its own standards for durable knowledge.

### nbertoa.com

`nbertoa.com` is the primary professional portfolio and the preferred first link when presenting my work. It should explain who I am, what I build, what I care about professionally, selected work, my technical and engineering direction, and clear paths to LinkedIn and GitHub.

The portfolio should be understandable without requiring visitors to explore the complete LifeOS repository. It should curate the most relevant information rather than reproduce everything available elsewhere. Its responsibility is clear presentation and selection, not archival completeness.

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn is the primary professional networking and career profile. It should communicate current role or professional description, experience, career progression, professional positioning, relevant skills, public professional activity, and networking identity.

It should not attempt to contain deep technical documentation. LinkedIn can point to the portfolio and GitHub when evidence or detail would improve understanding. Its role is to make the professional narrative easy for people and organizations to understand.

### GitHub

GitHub is the primary source of technical evidence. It should demonstrate code quality, project structure, engineering practices, technical interests, continuous learning, and selected public work. Repository quality and clarity matter more than repository count or activity streaks.

GitHub is not a complete résumé or biography. A small number of understandable, well-maintained repositories provides stronger evidence than a large collection of unfinished or unclear work. Project documentation should explain intent and decisions when that makes the work easier to evaluate.

## Public Positioning

My public presence should present me as a software engineer building toward specialization in Unreal Engine. That specialization is still evolving and should be supported by demonstrated work, learning, and engineering judgment rather than inflated titles.

The intended direction values robust and maintainable systems, critical thinking, defensive programming, Clean Code, SOLID, and Epic Games' recommended Unreal Engine practices. Architecture, debugging, code review, and thoughtful trade-offs are part of the technical capability the public presence should communicate.

Public positioning should reflect demonstrated work, current direction, and durable engineering values rather than inflated titles. Claims should remain specific enough to be verifiable and should evolve when evidence or direction changes.

## Cross-Platform Consistency

The following information should remain aligned where it is published: preferred professional name; current role or professional description; Unreal Engine specialization goal; main portfolio URL; primary GitHub and LinkedIn links; project names; employment dates; technology descriptions; biography facts; and contact information intentionally made public.

Wording may differ to suit the platform, but facts should not contradict one another. LifeOS should be consulted when durable professional principles or decisions need clarification. A platform update that changes a career fact, technical claim, or project description should be checked against its canonical source before publication.

## Content Principles

Publish work that provides evidence of judgment or capability. Prefer a small number of strong projects over many weak ones, explain decisions and trade-offs where useful, keep technical claims verifiable, and update or remove stale information.

Do not publish solely to appear active. Do not copy the same long-form content across every platform; link to the most appropriate canonical source instead. Make the next action clear for a visitor, whether that means viewing selected work, reading a project, connecting professionally, or reviewing technical evidence.

Public content should have a purpose: demonstrating skill, documenting a meaningful project, explaining an engineering decision, communicating professional progress, or helping another engineer. Content without a clear purpose does not need to be published.

## Privacy and Boundaries

Professional visibility does not require complete personal exposure. Do not publish sensitive personal details, private contact information unless intentionally designated for professional use, internal employer information, confidential project details, credentials, keys, infrastructure data, personal documents, unnecessary location or routine information, or opinions that do not belong in a professional context.

The absence of information is acceptable. Not every platform needs to be public or active, and no platform needs to contain every aspect of my work or life. Privacy, confidentiality, and professional judgment take priority over visibility.

## Maintenance

Review the professional presence when changing role, completing a significant project, changing professional direction, updating the portfolio, adding or retiring an official platform, finding contradictions between profiles, or noticing stale claims or broken links.

During a review, verify official links; role and experience dates; project descriptions; professional headline; portfolio availability; consistency between the portfolio, LinkedIn, and GitHub; and whether outdated content should be updated, archived, or removed. Maintenance should prioritize accuracy and clarity over constant activity.
